Cabin Project CIFA Channa striata Omics database

The CABIN Project – CIFA Channa Striata Omics Database is a comprehensive multi-omics resource integrating transcriptomics, metabolomics, and metagenomics data of Channa striata, focusing on genes associated with growth, reproduction, and disease resistance. It provides differential gene expression profiles, metabolic pathways, and gut microbiome composition to enhance understanding of physiological functions and health management in murrel. This database serves as a valuable tool for researchers and aquaculture scientists, facilitating genetic improvement, nutrigenomics applications, and disease management strategies in Channa striata aquaculture.

About the Species

Murrel (Channa species) is among the most important freshwater fish species in Asia. Known for its high nutritional value and medicinal properties, this species has significant economic importance in aquaculture. The database provides comprehensive genomic information to support research and development in murrel fish breeding and conservation.

About Cabin Project CIFA Channa striata Omics database

Cloud-Based Murrel Gene Annotation Hub (CMGAH) is a repository of transcriptome data of murrel species. The database contains various information for transcriptome sequence, differentially expressed genes of various tissues under varying conditions. This database is based on cloud architecture, developed in ASP.NET MVC and SQL SERVER.

FEATURES

Major components of CMGAH

SSRs

A collection of Simple Sequence Repeat (SSR) markers identified across the murrel transcriptome, along with their forward and reverse primer sequences.

DEG

A collection of Differentially Expressed Genes (DEGs) identified from tissues including secondary leaf, flag leaf and panicle under control and drought stress conditions.

Functional

A collection of all the transcripts of murrel from tissues, including secondary leaf, flag leaf and panicle under control and drought stress conditions obtained via RNA-Seq and assembly along with their functional annotations.

Pathway

A collection of biological pathways associated with murrel transcripts, providing insights into metabolic and regulatory networks.

Message of Principal Investigator

The Cloud-Based Murrel Gene Annotation Hub (CMGAH) was conceptualized upon completion of the transcriptome sequencing in 2023 with the aim of developing the resource database of cloud-based architecture. The database provides accurate and integrated gene related information. The database was developed with the view to provide information about most comprehensive part of the genome. The database contains various information for transcriptome sequence, functional annotation, microsatellite markers, DEGs and pathway information. The database is freely available resource provides researchers/scientists a portal to search, browse and query data to facilitate functional and applied OMICS studies in Murrel species.

- Dr. Principal Investigator
